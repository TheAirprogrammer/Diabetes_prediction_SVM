Facts & Questions

KNOW MORE

Parameters for Diabetes Prediction:
  1. Number of Pregnancies: This parameter is relevant for women as multiple pregnancies can increase the risk of gestational diabetes.
  2. Glucose Level (mg/dL): High glucose levels in the blood can indicate diabetes.
  3. Blood Pressure (mmHg): High blood pressure is often associated with diabetes and can be a risk factor for heart disease and stroke.
  4. Skin Thickness (mm): Increased skin thickness can be a sign of insulin resistance, which is common in type 2 diabetes.
  5. Insulin (mu U/ml): Low insulin levels or insulin resistance are key factors in diabetes.
  6. BMI (kg/m²): A high BMI (overweight or obesity) is a risk factor for type 2 diabetes.
  7. Diabetes Pedigree Function: This parameter assesses the family history of diabetes and genetic predisposition.
  8. Age (years): The risk of diabetes increases with age.
Facts about Diabetes:
  1. Over 400 million people worldwide have diabetes.
  2. Type 2 diabetes accounts for about 90% of all diabetes cases.
  3. Diabetes can lead to serious complications such as heart disease, stroke, kidney failure, and blindness.
  4. Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease where the body's immune system attacks and destroys insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.
  5. Diabetes can be managed through lifestyle changes, medication, and insulin therapy.
Calculate BMI (Body Mass Index​):

BMI=weight in kilograms/Square of height in meters

  1. Convert Height to Meters: If the height is in inches or feet, convert it to meters.

    • For example, if height is in inches, divide by 39.37 to get meters.
    • If height is in feet, multiply by 0.3048 to get meters.

  2. Calculate BMI: Once height is in meters, use the formula to calculate BMI by dividing weight (in kilograms) by the square of height (in meters).

Trained model for diabetes prediction:

Support Vector Machine (SVM) Model:

  • The model used for diabetes prediction is based on a Support Vector Machine (SVM) algorithm.
  • SVM is a supervised machine learning algorithm used for classification tasks, where it separates data points into different classes using a hyperplane.
  • SVM is particularly effective for binary classification tasks like diabetes prediction.

Diabetes Prediction

Welcome to Diabetes Prediction ToolOur Diabetes Prediction Tool is designed to help you assess your risk of diabetes based on key parameters such as pregnancy history, glucose level, blood pressure, BMI, and more. Powered by a Support Vector Machine (SVM) model, our tool provides accurate predictions to assist you in understanding your health status.

How It Works:
Simply enter your information into the form provided, including the number of pregnancies, glucose level, blood pressure, and other relevant details.
Our SVM model analyzes your input and provides a prediction indicating whether you are likely to have diabetes or not.
The prediction is based on patterns learned from a dataset of individuals with known diabetes status.

Why Use Our Tool:
Quick and easy: Get instant feedback on your diabetes risk in just a few clicks.
Informative: Gain insights into the factors influencing your diabetes risk and take proactive steps towards a healthier lifestyle.
